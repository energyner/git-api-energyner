Calories Burned While Walking. Health & Wellness

Algorithm to determine calorie expenditure for a person while walking or running.

To lose or gain 1 kilogram of body weight (2.205 lb), an approximate energy deficit or surplus of 7,000 to 7,700 calories (kcal) ( about 25875 to 30535 btu) is required. This figure is based on the energy stored in body fat.
Weight Loss: Creating a deficit of 7,000–7,700 kcal ( about 25875 to 30535 btu) results in a reduction of 1 kg (2.205 lb) of weight . Practical Example: If you reduce your daily intake by 500 kcal (1983 btu) , it will take you approximately 14 to 15 days to lose 1 kg (2.205 lb).
Key Distinction: In the context of nutrition, "calories" typically refers to kilocalories (kcal = 3.96566 btu).
